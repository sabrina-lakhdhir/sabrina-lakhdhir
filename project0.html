Project 0: The Leonard Manzara Button

Winter 2019

My group decided to create a button that represents Dr. Leonard Manzara.
We began by listing off some of the things we knew about him, and remembered about him from having taken courses taught by him. We also tried to pick out features which were distinct to his character.

The initial list of characteristics we began with was:
  • Use of overhead projector and plastic sheets when teaching
  • Teaches Assembly language (ARM)
  • Fan of Java
  • Mac supporter
  • Plaid, or solid black button down dress shirts
  • Dress shirts always opened one button "extra"
  • Almost always wears a vest
  • Calm, soothing, somewhat monotone voice when speaking
  • Often has coffee from Good Earth
  • Music background and some current research in music
  • Doesn't wear a wedding ring

    • Based off this list, we each came up with 10 initial design sketches. Most of my sketches revolved around the idea of involving a projector somehow.

    Initial Design Sketches:


    Unfortunately, we did not get a chance to speak to Dr. Manzara and gain more insight on his interests until immediately before our design critque. Due to this factor, only sketches 11 and 12 of my initial sketches really took into account what we leaert about him when we interviewed him.
    Some of the new things we learnt about him include:
  • Favourite colours: sage and crimson
  • Favourite band: The Beatles
  • Favourite movie: Pulp Fiction
  • Favourite genre of music: classical
  • Favourite shape: rectangle with rounded corners
  • Three words to describe himself: patient, analytical, ??
  • He was born and raised in Calgary
  • His undergrad is from the University of Calgary
  • He has a Masters and PhD in Music from State University in Buffalo, New York
  • He used to do work in signal processing, automated music, and text to speech
  • He plays guitar (acoustic and electric), keyboard, clarinet, sax, and oboe
  • He used to be in stage and concert bands

    • Following sharing these initial sketches with my group, as well as with the class during our design critique, some new details which my group decided were important to include were:
  • Ensuring there is something to solidify the projector being a key characteristic of Dr. Manzara, for example showing a course name or some code
    Keeping the idea of telling a story of two parts of Dr. Manzara's life

    • In my refined sketches, which were drawn following the in-class design critique, I made sure to include more details about where the button would be and what clicking the button would do. I also made sure to include more detail in my sketch by taking into account more facts which we learnt about Dr. Manzara during our interview.
